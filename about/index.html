About Chandler Mahaney

Engineers automate repetitive work by default. Most hotel teams have never had one in the room who looked at their morning routine and asked why a person was doing it.

That's the whole idea here. The scripts, the scheduled jobs, the data pipelines — these are basic infrastructure in any software team. They're invisible in most hotel operations. Bringing those tools into the daily reporting workflow doesn't require a big technology project. It requires someone who knows how to do it and cares about getting it right. Once the assembly is automated, the AI layer on top of it actually becomes useful instead of aspirationally useful.

How I work with a team.

The first project is always narrow — one workflow, one output, done fast enough that the team can see it working before they have to take it on faith.

1
Map the actual workflow, not the ideal one.

What reports exist, who pulls them, what format they come in, what manual steps happen between the source and the finished output, and who uses it.

2
Automate the assembly — work from what already exists.

Most of this starts from scheduled exports the team is already producing. No new systems required, no IT project, no PMS API negotiation.

3
Once the output is reliable, add the intelligence layer.

A clean, consistent, on-time data feed is what makes AI interpretation actually work. Get that first. Then it's worth building on top of.

Start with one workflow.

Tell me what you're building by hand every morning, who uses the output, and where the pain is. I'll tell you honestly whether there's a fast win and what it looks like.
